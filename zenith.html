Welcome to Xenith

Xenith is a department festival celebrated once in two years.

The theme for Xenith 2020 was “Glitches to Perfection” and was scheduled for 17 and 18 January. During the two days, several events were organized.

Xenith 2020 marked its beginning by inaugurating the exhibition titled A Glitch in Time by the respected Principal Dr. Rajendra Shinde. An exhibition showcasing the IT project work of the students was open to everyone for the entire day.

The projects displayed included:

  • A Dancing Robot
  • Unity-based Games
  • A model Rocket
  • Gesture Recognition Sensors
  • A 3D Printer
  • And much more

The first talk of the fest was conducted by Dr. Paritosh K. Pandya, an Indian Computer Scientist and a Researcher at TIFR, on the topic “Intelligent Processing of Indian Music: where technology meets music”.

This highly interesting and well-received session discussed applying DSP and machine learning techniques to Indian classical music.

Later, Xenith was honoured to host Dr. Subhasis Chauduri, Director of IIT Bombay, for his talk: “Higher Education: the perspective of an Administrator”. This provided students with a broader and better perspective on pursuing higher studies.

Day 1 Events

Besides educational talks, Day 1 included exciting competitions involving students from various Mumbai colleges.

Bot The Builder, a gameshow-style event, challenged players’ wit, logic, and teamwork. Teams of two metaphorically built their “robot” using clues, puzzles, and collaboration.

A treasure hunt titled Glitch Don't Hitch was also held for the adventurous. Participants faced relaxing draining physical challenges and brain teasers across Fort, Mumbai.

Day 2 Events

The second day featured the thrilling Laser Tag Event, which was a huge success and saw massive participation from the student community.

A workshop by GreyAtom titled “Kung Fu Data With Pandas!” introduced attendees to Pandas (Panel Data) — a Python library for data analysis.

Attendees learned to use Pandas for tasks like data cleaning, pre-processing, and extracting insights, gaining hands-on experience with real data.

The final talk of the fest, “Mapping Perfection: A Journey Through 3 Case Studies”, was delivered by Mr. Aashish Sharma, Co-Founder of Enliten IT India. He shared his entrepreneurial story, highlighting the journey from glitches to perfection.

Closing Ceremony

The two-day fest concluded with the distribution of certificates and prizes to all participants and volunteers by Prof. Rachana Pandey, Staff-In-Charge of Xenith'20.

A heartfelt closing note was given by Mr. Chirag Jain, student of TYIT and Chairperson of Xenith'20, thanking everyone who contributed to its success.


— Xavier's IT Department
